Sailor Moon
This is the first anime I really got into. I had seen others like some Dragonball, Speedracer, and Gotchaman before hand but Sailor Moon was significantly different. It's one of the first shows I saw that had a major story arc and you could watch the characters grow over time. On top of that the artwork really stood out. Most of the artwork for American cartoons always looked so bad.

Hunt: Showdown
This is probably one of my most recent major interests but I have about 4.5k hours in this game over the past 6ish years. This game has a different feel than most other FPS because it takes place in the late 1800s and has a design that really fits that aesthetic. The gun play really benefits from this because it has older guns take a lot more time to shoot and reload so the fights take a bit longer and each shot really feels like it matters. One extra thing I appreciate is that most of the DLC skins all fit with the aesthetic. Too many games do collabs with other properties or just try too hard to make their characters look bad ass. In hunt most of the characters look like they've been living in that world.

Zero Player Game
Okay, this one is weird. It's a game concept I always come back to. It may sound like a clicker game but it's far from it. In this genre, you create a character and it's AI takes over and plays the game for you. Sometimes you have some small ability to change what it can or cannot do. My current attempt at making a zero player game has gone pretty well. I'm adding a whole new level of difficulty to creating it because I always want it work a bit like a multiplayer game too. You create a character, it does it's own thing but it can interact with other peoples characters.
